# 👥 HR Attrition Analysis Dashboard — Excel

## 📌 Overview
An interactive HR Attrition dashboard built entirely in Microsoft Excel, analyzing employee attrition data to help HR teams understand why employees leave and which groups are most at risk. This project demonstrates end-to-end analytics — from raw data to a fully interactive dashboard — within Excel.

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies
![Excel](https://img.shields.io/badge/Microsoft_Excel-217346?style=for-the-badge&logo=microsoft-excel&logoColor=white)

---

## 🎯 Objectives
- Calculate overall attrition rate and track key HR KPIs
- Identify high-risk departments, age groups, and salary bands
- Analyze attrition by job role, education, gender, and years at company
- Provide actionable recommendations to reduce employee turnover

## 📊 Dashboard Features

| Visual | Description |
|---|---|
| 🎯 KPI Cards | Total Employees, Attrition Count, Attrition Rate, Avg Age, Avg Salary |
| 🎓 Attrition by Education | Donut chart breakdown by education field |
| 👶 Attrition by Age Group | Bar chart showing which age groups leave most |
| 💰 Attrition by Salary Slab | Horizontal bar chart — entry-level vs senior |
| 💼 Attrition by Job Role | Table with attrition counts per role |
| 📅 Attrition by Years at Company | Line chart showing tenure vs attrition trend |

## 🔑 Key Insights
- Employees with 0–2 years at the company show the highest attrition rates
- Entry-level salary brackets contribute disproportionately to turnover
- Certain job roles are identified as high-risk for attrition

---

## 💡 Recommendations
- Invest in onboarding and early engagement programs for new hires
- Review compensation structures for entry-level positions
- Conduct stay interviews for high-risk job roles to identify pain points
